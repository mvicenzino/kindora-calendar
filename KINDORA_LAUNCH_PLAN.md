## Week 1 — Seed & Soft Launch (Mar 23–29)

**Goal: Get your first 10 paying users. Prioritize warm audiences.**

---

### Monday, Mar 23 — Friends & Personal Network

**Action:** Text or DM 20–30 people personally. Not a mass email — individual messages.

**Message template:**
> "I built something I think you'd actually find useful. Would you try it and tell me honestly what you think? It's $7/month but I'll comp you the first month — just want real feedback."

- Target people who: have kids AND aging parents, work in healthcare, or are caregivers
- Set up a simple referral tracker (a Google Sheet is fine)
- Goal: 5–10 signups from personal network by end of week

---

### Tuesday–Wednesday, Mar 25–26 — Reddit

Post in one subreddit per day. **Story-first, product second. Include a screenshot. Mention the free trial.**

| Subreddit | Angle |
|---|---|
| **r/AgingParents** | "I built a tool to coordinate care for my mom with my siblings — here's what I learned" |
| **r/CaregiverSupport** | Most targeted — be direct, share the product, mention it's in beta |
| **r/Parenting** | "Anyone else drowning in coordinating kids + aging parents at the same time?" |
| **r/Mommit** or **r/daddit** | Family scheduling chaos angle |
| **r/eldercare** | Reserve for Week 2 |

**Post format:**
1. Open with a personal story (2–3 sentences)
2. Describe the problem clearly
3. Share what you built and why
4. Link + free trial mention
5. End with a genuine question to drive comments

**Important:** Respond to every comment — Reddit rewards engagement and it builds trust.

---

### Thursday, Mar 27 — X (Twitter)

**Post a launch thread:**

> 1/ I'm launching Kindora today — a family OS I built because I was drowning caring for my kids AND my aging parents at the same time. Here's what it does 🧵
>
> 2/ [Screenshot: calendar view with family members]
>
> 3/ [Screenshot: Kira AI advisor conversation]
>
> 4/ [Screenshot: medication tracker]
>
> 5/ Beta is $7/month. Link in bio. Would love early feedback from anyone in the sandwich generation.

**After posting:**
- Follow and engage with accounts in eldercare, caregiving, and parenting spaces
- Reply to conversations about caregiver burnout — don't just broadcast
- Search hashtags: #sandwichgeneration #caregiving #eldercare #familycaregiver

---

### Friday–Sunday, Mar 28–30 — LinkedIn

**Post a founder story** (Friday):

> I'm 35, I have two kids, and my mom was just diagnosed with dementia. Here's what I built.

- Make it personal and real — no startup hype
- Tell the story of the problem before mentioning the product
- End with: "We're live at kindora.ai — $7/month beta. Would love for you to try it."
- Tags: #caregiving #sandwichgeneration #familytech #startup #eldercare

**This is likely your highest-engagement post of Week 1.**

## Week 2 — Community Push (Mar 30–Apr 5)

**Goal: Find your community channels. Scale to 25–50 users.**

---

### Monday–Tuesday, Mar 30–31 — Facebook Groups

**Join and post in these group types:**

| Group Type | Search Terms |
|---|---|
| Caregiver support | "caregiver support group" (50K+ member groups exist) |
| Sandwich generation | "sandwich generation" |
| Senior care | "aging parents" / "senior care" |
| Working parents | "working moms" / "working parents" |

**Post format:**
- Open with a question: *"Is anyone else managing care for parents AND kids at the same time? How do you handle it?"*
- Share a personal story
- Mention Kindora naturally, include a link
- Avoid copy-paste across groups — customize each one

---

### Wednesday, Apr 1 — Reddit Double Down

Post in new subreddits:
- **r/eldercare**
- **r/SandwichGeneration** (small but highly targeted)
- **r/FamilyMedicine**

Also: go back and respond to every comment from Week 1 posts. This bumps your posts back up in visibility.

---

### Thursday, Apr 3 — LinkedIn Article

Write a 400–500 word article (not just a post — use LinkedIn's article format):

**Suggested title:** *"What the 'sandwich generation' actually needs — and why no software solved it until now"*

**Structure:**
1. Paint the picture of the problem (2–3 paragraphs)
2. What you tried that didn't work (existing tools, why they fell short)
3. What you built and the core insight behind it
4. CTA: try Kindora free for 14 days

Articles get significantly more reach than regular posts and are indexed by search.

---

### All of Week 2 — X Engagement Mode

- Reply to people talking about caregiver burnout, sandwich generation stress, family scheduling
- Share one insight or tip per day (not always product-related — build credibility first)
- Repost anyone who mentions Kindora
- Search and engage: "caring for aging parent", "sandwich generation", "caregiver burnout"

---

## Week 3 — Proof & Push (Apr 6–12)

**Goal: Social proof, word-of-mouth loop. Push toward 50–100 users.**

---

### Monday, Apr 6 — Ask for Testimonials

Email or DM every user from Weeks 1–2:

> "Would you share one sentence about how Kindora has helped you? I'd love to feature it on the site and in my posts."

Use testimonials immediately — add them to the homepage and include in posts this week.

---

### Tuesday–Wednesday, Apr 7–8 — Product Hunt Launch (Optional but High-Impact)

Submit Kindora to Product Hunt. Best days are Tuesday or Wednesday.

**Prepare:**
- Tagline: *"The family OS for the sandwich generation"*
- 3–5 screenshots (calendar, Kira, medication tracker, documents vault)
- A personal founder comment explaining who it's for and why you built it
- Ask all current users to upvote on launch day (DM them individually)

**Day-of:** Post on all channels asking for support. Be present in the comments all day.

---

### Wednesday–Thursday, Apr 8–9 — Referral Push

Email all current users:

> "You've been using Kindora for a couple weeks now — thank you. If you know another family dealing with the same chaos you were, send them this link. If they sign up, you both get a free month."

Use a simple Stripe coupon code or just honor it manually at this stage.

---

### Friday, Apr 10 — Wrap-Up Post on All Channels

Share what you've learned publicly:

> "Two weeks since launch — here's what surprised me."

Include:
- Number of users (even if small — honesty builds trust)
- Most common thing people say they love
- Something you're changing based on feedback
- A thank you to early users

**These authentic reflection posts consistently outperform product posts.**
